Sketch Grammar of Luganda

Conducted semester-long primary linguistic fieldwork with a native Luganda speaker over 14 elicitation sessions to produce a complete sketch grammar, documenting 21 consonants, 17 noun classes, 9 tense distinctions, and a full syntactic analysis of word order and question formation in an understudied Bantu language.

Conjunctions and Disjunctions in Tiriki

Documented coordination in Tiriki (a Bantu macro-language of Western Kenya) via native speaker elicitation, analyzing the syntactic distribution and semantic distinctions of conjunctions, disjunctions, and subordinate clauses with attention to tense-conditioned morphological variation in coordinated verb phrases.

Spring 2026 · Topics in Morphosyntax, Pomona College

Nominalization in Tiriki

Co-authored a morphosyntactic analysis of nominalization in Tiriki, identifying 8–9 nominalizing prefixes spanning agentive, instrumental, gerund, and infinitive noun classes and systematically characterizing their verbal vs. nominal properties across auxiliary-taking, object-marking, and derivational suffix compatibility.
